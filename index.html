This section aims to give an overview of The Gotita Project, the current situation of WASH assignments in the World and Colombia, and how we will tackle the problem.

Problem Statement

In 2017, an estimated 1.2 million people died as a result of unsafe water sources. This was 2.2% of global deaths.

For this reason, multiple NGOs are now trying to improve this aspect and improve the quality of life of those who lived in undeserved areas. Nevertheless, there are too much challenges that WASH units are facing now.

Experiences from the field

"Right now, in refugee camps, water monitoring it is a costly, time consuming and inefficient process. To measure water quality we need a high number of people and during rainy seasons we need to transport them in helicopter. Furthermore, data is being taken by hand, which is highly time consuming."

WASH Advisor for a Swiss NGO in Nigeria

"People from San Isidro of Puerto Caldas in Colombia have recurrently problems with water quality due to illegal embezzlement of water networks. Our company is only ensuring good water quality to the people that is currently subscribe to our services."

President of the public water company of Pereira

Challenges faced ensuring water quality

To ensure water quality we need to:

  • Measure some parameters of water quality at least twice a day in risk areas, and once a day in more safe ones.

Too many changes are faced ensuring water quality, even though we agree that:

  1. Rural zones are the most affected ones. The more distant they are from cities, the worst water quality will be.
  2. Its difficult to ensure water quality with manual techniques in a daily basis.
  3. Measuring water quality can require a lot of ressources and costly.

Our solution

We aim with this project to design a device that can aloud either public utility companies, households or WAHS teams from NGOs, to measure at distance and automatically water quality.
